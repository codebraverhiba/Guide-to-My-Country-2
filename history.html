-Ancient Rome (753 BC-476 AD)

  • Italy was the center of the Roman Empire, one of the greatest civilizations in history.
  • Rome developed law, architecture, roads, literature, and art that shaped Western civilization.
  • Landmarks like the Colosseum, Pantheon, and Roman Forum still stand today.

-Middle Ages (500-1400)

  • After Rome’s fall, Italy split into many city-states (like Venice, Florence, and Milan).
  • The Catholic Church, based in Vatican City, became a major power.
  • Art, learning, and religion were closely connected during this time.

-Renaissance (1400–1600)

  • Italy became the birthplace of the Renaissance, a period of creativity and discovery.
  • Great artists and thinkers like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael transformed art and science.
  • Cities like Florence and Rome were cultural centers of Europe.

-Unification of Italy (1800s)

  • Italy was divided for centuries into kingdoms and states.
  • In the 1860s, leaders like Giuseppe Garibaldi and Count Cavour helped unite the country into one nation (1861).

-20th Century

  • Italy experienced World War I and II, and the rule of Benito Mussolini under Fascism.
  • After WWII, Italy became a republic in 1946.
  • The nation quickly rebuilt and became a global center for fashion, design, and film.
